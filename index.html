What is Netflix?

Netflix is a subscription streaming service that allows its members to watch series and movies on a device with an internet connection. Depending on your plan, you can also download TV shows and movies to an iOS, Android, or Windows 10 device and watch them offline. If you're already a member and want to learn more about how to use Netflix, visit how to use Netflix.

  • Series and films

    Netflix content varies by region and may also vary over time. You can see a variety of:

    1. documentaries.
    2. films.
    3. series.
    4. Award-winning Netflix original content and much more.

    The more content you watch, the better Netflix can recommend shows and movies for you.

Compatible devices

You can watch Netflix through any internet-connected device that has the Netflix app, including smart TVs, game consoles, media players, set-top boxes, smartphones, and tablets. You can also watch Netflix on your computer, in an internet browser. For information on supported web browsers, see the system requirements, as well as our Internet connection speed recommendations for best performance.



  1. There may be a small percentage of devices that are not compatible with all plans.

  2. The Netflix app may come pre-installed on certain devices; in other cases, you will need to download it. The functionality of the Netflix app may vary from device to device.

Plans and prices

Each Netflix plan determines the number of devices you can watch Netflix content on at the same time and whether you can watch in High Definition (HD), Full High Definition (FHD), or Ultra High Definition (UHD). You can change plans or cancel online service at any time.

To start watching Netflix:

  1. Visit here
  2. Choose your plan
  3. Create an account entering your email and password
  4. Enter a payment method

As a Netflix member, you will be charged once a month on your subscription date.

How to keep your account safe?

These are our top recommendations for keeping your account and personal information safe.

Using a password for Netflix that you don't use for other purposes

Requirements for your password

  • It must be different from the one you use for other websites or other apps.
  • Containing a minimum of 8 characters.
  • which includes a combination of upper and lower case letters, numbers, and symbols.
  • That cannot be easily guessed, such as "password", "12345678", or personal information (name, birthday, address).

A password manager can help you sort out your unique passwords.

You can update your password from the Account page or send yourself an email or text message to reset your password.

Other measures you should take

  • Sign out of unused or unrecognized devices
  • Be alert to fake messages and websites (phishing)
  • Report fraudulent or suspicious activity
  • Add a phone number for password recovery
  • Keep your computer protected
    • Unusual slow or sluggish response from the computer.
    • Advertisements or pop-ups during streaming.
    • Unexpected reboots, interruptions, or crashes.
    • Ad-Aware (Windows)
    • Sophos (Windows, Mac)
    • Trend Micro (Windows, Mac)
    • F-Secure (Windows, Mac)
    • Norton (Windows, Mac)
    • Malwarebytes (Windows)
